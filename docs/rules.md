# Uno Chess: Official Rulebook

Welcome to **Uno Chess**, a chaotic, fast-paced hybrid tabletop game that fuses the strategic spatial movement of traditional Chess with the unpredictable, hand-managing card mechanics of Uno.

The game is a race on two parallel tracks: you are playing a parallel Uno game and a parallel Chess game simultaneously to see which player can win at least one of the games first.

---

## 1. Component Setup

- **The Board & Pieces:** Set up a standard 8x8 chessboard with all 32 pieces in their traditional starting positions.
- **The Uno Deck:** Shuffle a standard 108-card Uno deck. Deal a hand of **7 cards** to each player. Place the remaining deck face down to form the Draw Pile. Turn the top card face up to begin the Discard Pile.
- **Determining Turns:** White plays first. To initiate a turn, a player must be able to legally play a card from their hand.

---

## 2. Fundamental Turn Mechanics

Every turn is driven by the Uno cards. You cannot move a chess piece without playing a card, and you cannot play a card without fulfilling its chess-move requirements.

1. **Card Matching:** On your turn, you must play a card from your hand onto the Discard Pile. The card must match the top card of the Discard Pile by **Color**, **Number**, or **Symbol**.
2. **Drawing Cards:** If you cannot play a matching card, you must draw one card from the Draw Pile. If the drawn card is playable, you may play it immediately; otherwise, your turn ends, and you make no chess moves.
3. **No Stacking:** House rules for stacking cards (e.g., placing a +2 on a +2) are strictly prohibited. In Uno Chess, drawing/action cards grant powerful board benefits rather than offensive penalties, so stacking is not allowed.

---

## 3. Card Actions & Chess Movement

The card you play dictates the exact actions you must or can take on the chessboard. All chess pieces move according to traditional chess rules (e.g., Knights jump, Pawns move forward, etc.).

### A. Number Cards (0–9)

The number on the card dictates exactly how many **consecutive chess moves** you must execute on that turn.

- **Multi-Move Comboing:** If you play a **4**, you must make 4 separate chess moves back-to-back before your turn ends. You can move the same piece multiple times or move completely different pieces.
- **The Checkmate Intercept:** You cannot move "through" a checkmate. If you deliver a definitive checkmate mid-combo (e.g., on move 2 of a 5-move card), **your turn ends immediately**. You do not need to fully resolve the remaining actions on the card, and you win the game.
- **Moving Through Check:** You cannot end a single sub-move in an illegal self-check state unless a subsequent move in your exact same turn sequence gets your King out of danger.

### B. Draw Two (+2) Cards

When you play a **+2 card**, you trigger a special "Resurrection" mechanic instead of taking standard board moves:

- You are allowed to **bring back exactly 2 of your captured pieces** (including your Queen) from outside the board.
- **Placement Rule:** You cannot place a resurrected piece on the opponent's side of the board. You can place them on **any empty square within your own side of the board** (Rows 1–4 for White, Rows 5–8 for Black).
- _Note: If you have no pieces captured, the resurrection effect is wasted, though the card still alters the active color/symbol flow._

### C. Wild Draw Four (+4) Cards

When you play a **Wild Draw 4 card**, you change the active color and trigger a massive army reinforcement:

- You are allowed to **bring back exactly 4 of your captured pieces** (including your Queen) from outside the board.
- **Placement Rule:** You cannot place resurrected pieces on the opponent's side of the board. They must be placed on **any empty squares within your own side of the board** (Rows 1–4 for White, Rows 5–8 for Black).

### D. Reverse Cards

- Playing a Reverse card changes the tactical flow. In a 2-player game, it acts as a mirror modifier, reversing the turn order back to you or forcing a directional shift.
- **The Check Bypass:** It is **entirely legal** to play a Reverse card even if it leaves your opponent in an inescapable check. Preventing them from reacting to a threat is part of the fun.

### E. Skip Cards

- Playing a Skip card immediately cancels your opponent's ability to play a card on their next turn, passing the action right back to you.
- **The Inescapable Check:** It is **entirely legal** to play a Skip card to deliberately lock an opponent's King in check without giving them a turn to escape.

---

## 4. Winning the Game

Because this is a parallel race across both formats, a player wins the second they achieve victory in _either_ game track:

1. **The Chess Victory (Checkmate or Capture):** Achieving a board state where the opponent’s King is checkmated or physically captured. If a King is captured, the game ends instantly and that player loses; a King can never be resurrected because the game is already over.
2. **The Uno Victory (Going Out):** You win the **exact second your final card leaves your hand**. You must yell **"Uno!"** when you have only 1 card remaining. The moment your last card hits the discard pile, the game is over and you win instantly—you do not need to execute any of the chess moves dictated by that final card.
